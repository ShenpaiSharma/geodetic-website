The Relevance of Biology to Pharmacy Students

Pharmacy students frequently ask the following questions at the start of his/her university education - Is biology important in pharmacy education?; As someone without a strong biology background, will I stumble in the pharmacy programme? etc. Defined as the science of life and living organisms, Biology cross-talks with Pharmacy on many platforms since the profession involves everything about ensuring the appropriate and safe administration of medical drugs into human patients. This short interactive masterclass will zoom in on the biological molecules that make up a living animal cell - how these different molecules allow a cell in a body to carry out the normal daily functions, as well as how they provide weak links, including being attacked by infectious agents and getting mutated to non-functional types. The class concludes by providing a glimpse of what pharmacy professionals can do to these biological molecules, to design, develop and administer medical drugs for the treatment of diseases.

Reflections: A Professional Journey

Amidst a busy life, it is easy to forget that the path we took and the process we experienced can teach us the most. It is also the most enjoyable. What does a professional journey look like? Is it a job, career or vocation? Would it be a long voyage, marked by discovery, pleasure, adventures, and full of knowledge and wonderful accomplishments? Catch a peek of what pharmacy practice is and what matters at the heart of all we do.

Profile

Associate Professor Chew Eng Hui
Department of Pharmacy
Faculty of Science

Assoc Professor Chew Eng Hui received her BSc (Pharmacy) from NUS and her doctorate in drug discovery at the University of Nottingham. After spending a few years as a postdoctoral fellow in Karolinska Institute in Sweden, she moved back to Singapore and has since accumulated more than 10 years of teaching experience in the Department of Pharmacy. She currently teaches biochemistry and biotechnology related topics in modules offered in the Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Science undergraduate programmes. She is the module coordinator for the essential module PR1152 Pharmacy Foundations: Science and Therapeutics I, offered to Year 1 Pharmacy students enrolled in the Pharmacy Undergraduate Program. As a Pharmacy alumna, Prof Chew is passionate about imparting knowledge to undergraduates, especially with the focus on healthcare and pharmaceutical science professionals during the pandemic.

Profile

Associate Professor Lita Chew
Department of Pharmacy
Faculty of Science

Assoc Professor Lita Chew received her pharmacy degree from NUS and her post-graduate masters in medical science in Clinical Oncology at University of Birmingham. She completed her fellowship training in Pharmacy Practice at the University Illinois Chicago. Prof Chew is an influential leader in the healthcare sector, with over 25 years of practice experience, and her work impacts the pharmacy workforce and pharmaceutical landscape in Singapore. She has guided the establishment of register for specialist pharmacists, the training and development framework for pharmacist specialists, national pharmacy residency training, the review of pharmacist and pharmacy technician career paths an the development of competency frameworks for advanced pharmacy practice pharmacists and pharmacy technicians. She also led the development of the National Pharmacy Strategy, a 10-year plan to transform the pharmaceutical landscape in Singapore. She currently teaches pharmacy practice, pharmacoeconomics and oncology therapeutics related topics in the Pharmacy undergraduate and PharmD modules.

Profile

Associate Professor Chui Wai Keung
Department of Pharmacy
Faculty of Science

Assoc Prof Chui Wai Keung received his BSc (Pharmacy) from NUS and his PhD from Aston University in Birmingham. He has been involved in pharmacy education and pharmaceutical science research since 1991 at NUS. Previously Head of the Department of Pharmacy (2013 to 2015), Prof Chui was one of five founding Co-Chairpersons of the NUS interprofessional education steering committee. He is currently a council member of the Singapore Pharmacy Council. He led the pharmacy entrustable professional activity (EPA) workgroup that initiated the use of EPA for pre-registration training for pharmacists. He co-leads the design of the new competency-based B.Pharm. (Hons) programme which integrates basic, clinical and systems sciences as its hallmark. He sits on working committees from the World Health Organization and the Western Pacific Pharmaceutical Forum.

Preparing Pharmacists for the Innovative New World of Healthcare

The healthcare sector is staying responsive to new challenges, such as keeping Singapores growing elderly population healthy in the community and out of hospital. The optimal use of medicines is key to addressing this challenge. Pharmacists, as the health professionals with expertise in medicines, play a critical role. In our presentation, we will showcase pharmacists multidimensional roles. We will then demonstrate how we prepare our students through our innovative, student-centric integrated professional programme, including how our students engage in team-based learning for engaging class experiences. We will also highlight the career opportunities that await you upon registration as a pharmacist. If you would like to make a difference to pharmaceutical care, join us!

Zooming In and Out of the Singapore Healthcare System

Our Singapore healthcare system is evolving to brave the challenges of an ageing tsunami with a shrinking population, rapid advancements in medicine, artificial intelligence, ballooning healthcare costs and the COVID-19 pandemic. Discover how pharmacy is a critical component of this healthcare landscape and how it has transformed alongside our evolving healthcare system to one that embraces cutting-edge medicine and technology. Find out the crucial roles that pharmacists play at the micro, meso and macro levels, leading to the safe delivery of high quality services.

Profile

Professor Paul Gallagher
Deputy Head (Clinical)
Department of Pharmacy

Prof Paul Gallagher obtained his MBA and Ph.D. from University College London and Trinity College Dublin, respectively. He was previously the Head of Pharmacy of the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (RCSI) (2010-2018), where he reformed pharmacy education through his Directorship of the National Pharmacy Internship Programme and the establishment of the Irish Institute of Pharmacy. Profile

Associate Professor Camilla Wong
Director, Allied Health, Sengkang General Hospital
Chief Pharmicist, Ministry of Health
Adjunct Associate Professor, Department of Pharmacy, NUS

Assoc Prof Camilla Wong has 25 years of experience in healthcare. She is currently Director of Allied Health at Sengkang General Hospital (SKH) where she has an integral role in planning the infrastructure, processes and services for the hospital and its Allied Health service, and Deputy Group Director of Group Allied Health services, SingHealth (SHS) overseeing talent development. She concurrently holds the position of Chief Pharmacist at the Ministry of Health (MOH).

Assoc Prof Wong has contributed significantly to the development of career pathways and competency frameworks for pharmacists and Allied Health professionals both cluster-wide and nationally. A recognised leader in healthcare, she sits on numerous committees across different portfolios, including the SHS Allied Health Council, One Rehabilitation Workgroup, Institute of Patient Safety and Quality and Patient Experience Council, the Singapore Pharmacy Council, the MOH Pharmacist Specialist Accreditation Board, and the Health and Sciences Authority Product Vigilance Advisory Committee.

She is a past president of the Pharmaceutical Society of Singapore (PSS) and has received various awards, including the PSSs Professor Lucy Wan, Outstanding Pharmacist of the Year Award, Federation of Asian Pharmaceutical Associations Ishidate Award for Hospital Pharmacy, Group CEO SingHealth Excellence Award for Allied Health and the Singapore National Day Awards (Commendation Medal).

Profile

Professor Christina Chai

Prof Christina Chai obtained her B.Sc. (Hons) from the University of Canterbury and her Ph.D. in synthetic organic chemistry from the Research School of Chemistry, Australian National University. Following her Ph.D., she was awarded a Violette and Samuel Glasstone Research Fellowship at the University of Oxford where she studied bioorganic reaction mechanisms.
